Aims of this report

This report seeks to describe the 2025 CIFTR data in greater detail and clarify the methods by which analyses and visualizations were created. It is meant to be reviewed after viewing the primary material.

About the Data

Survey Design and Collection

This survey is a long running census of all of the stores, restaurants, bars, and other establishments in the city of Cleveland. It aims to collect information on the accessibility to a variety of products, including healthy foods, as well as drugs and other general products.

The information on these establishments is collected annually during the summer by a team of volunteers. The details are collected primarily via groundtruthing.

Tobacco Sales

Establishments that sold tobacco products were counted and are displayed below. Tobacco products considered include the following:

  • Cigarettes

  • Menthol cigarettes

  • ENDS (e-cigarettes)

  • Cigarillos/Little Cigars

  • Nicotine Pouches

  • Roll-Your-Own Tobacco

The following establishments are omitted from this list, as described previously:

  • Restaurants (Full-service and Limited-service/Fast-food)

  • Bars and Liquor Stores

  • Closed Stores/Restaurants

  • Tobacco/Smoking Suppliers

In order to do this, we define a new variable has_tobacco, which is a binary variable, in which 1 means the establishment in question has one of the above defined products.

Then, because this report is concerned primarily with easily accessible sources of tobacco, certain establishments are removed. Closed establishments were removed, along with restaurants and bars, as well as tobacco suppliers. Stores that seemed to primarily sell tobacco (based on signage for a smoke or e-cigarette shop, or other exterior signs) were not further investigated, and simply assumed to carry some form of tobacco or another.

Tobacco 21

Using this data, we are able to determine how many of the stores that do sell tobacco have proper local, state, or federal Tobacco 21 signage.

Here, we used our cleaned data set and established a binary variable t21, with a 1 meaning that local, state, or federal T21 signage was posted. A value of 1 means that somewhere on the premises signage complying with either local, state, or federal tobacco 21 policies were placed. A value of 0 means that this was not the case.

It was considered whether to omit observations with missing data on t21. The decision was made not to do so in order to keep the data set pristine for future analyses in this report. The majority of observations missing this data did not sell tobacco, save for one observation.

Ad Coverage

Using the data, we can determine the number of establishments that had more than 25% of their doors and windows covered by advertisements.

We established a new binary variable ad25, for which a value of 1 means that 25% or more of the windows and doors were covered by advertisements, while a value of 0 means that this was not the case.

A large number of establishments were missing data for this variable, with no obviously discernible pattern among them.

Maps

As a small note about the maps that can be viewed at https://tinyurl.com/CIFTR2025Map, the information displayed comes directly from this data set. Given the street addresses of the establishments, a geocoder was used to calculate the latitude and longitude of each location for placement in GIS. Much of the information, including establishment type, whether or not the establishment sold tobacco or had t21 signage, more was calculated exactly as above before being geocoded.
